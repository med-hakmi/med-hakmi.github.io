COVID-19@MP2D

The ongoing COVID-19 pandemic is caused by a new virus strain of the Coronaviridae family known as SARS-CoV-2, an enveloped betacoronavirus with a single-stranded, positive-sense RNA. Understanding how this coronavirus diverts the host during infection is critical to the implementation of effective therapeutic strategies involving the development of new drugs or the readaptation of existing ones to counter this COVID-19 outbreak.

WE SUPPORT DRUG DISCOVERY EFFORTS TO COMBAT COVID-19 

OUR CONTRIBUTIONS

In a previous study, we identified some HIV and HCV protease inhibitor drugs that could also work for SARS-CoV-2 since they showed adequate binding modes within the active site of its main protease (as shown in the image bellow). Further bioassays are required to confirm those results.
We are currently working on other projects in collaboration with the genomics unit of our laboratory in order to detect weak points of the virus and tackle it in a more adequate and effective way.
